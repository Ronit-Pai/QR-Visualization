How QR Codes Work

An illustrated guide to encoding, structure, and error correction

QR codes are grids of dark and light modules with special patterns that help scanners detect, align, and decode the data.

Your text is encoded into bits, then arranged into 8-bit codewords. Depending on the mode (numeric, alphanumeric, byte), the capacity and efficiency vary.

These blocks are interleaved to improve robustness against localized damage.

QR codes add parity codewords so that even if part of the code is damaged, the original data can be recovered. Levels L/M/Q/H provide 7–30% recoverability.

A scanner locates finder patterns, aligns perspective, reads timing patterns to determine module size, then decodes data and applies error correction to retrieve the original content.
